About the University

Khallikote Unitary University is a newly constituted institution of national repute offering Graduate, Post-Graduate and M.Phil. Courses in Arts, Commerce, and Science along with Self-Financing Courses. The Government of Odisha has conferred the status of the University with effect from 1st August 2021. In an attempt to maintain its distinctiveness and brilliance, the University is on constant strive to formulate curricular, co-curricular, and extra-curricular activities so that the students would become self-reliant and can contribute to nation-building activities.

Admission Procedure :

For admission into any of the self finance courses the candidate has to appear at Entrance Examination as per the programme notified and he/she will be considered for admission on the basis of his/her performance in the Entrance Examination and his /her academic score taken together. Admission into UG courses will be based on merit.

RESERVATION OF SEATS: (As per SAMS guideline)

  1. 16.25% of the total numbers of sanctioned seats of a course are reserved for candidates belonging to SC category.

  2. 22.50 of the total number of sanctioned seats of a course are reserved for candidates belonging to ST category

  3. The seats of SC and ST are not interchangeable and any reserved seats remaining vacant in either category will be filled up from the general merit, list.

  4. 2 % of the total seats shall be kept reserved for children of bonafide Burma Repatriates B.R.).

  5. 1 % of the total number of seats shall be kept reserved for the children/wife/husband/ of Ex-Servicemen (ESM).

  6. 1 % of the total number of seats shall be kept reserved for the children/wife/husband of Serving Defense Personnel (SDP).

  7. 3 % of total number of seats at the Degree and Post-Graduate level shall be kept reserved for disabled (DBL) persons (minimum 40% disability to be certified by the CDMO/ SDMO/Chief Medical Officer, City Hospital, Berhampur).

  8. 2 % of the total seats will be reserved for the children/ grand children of Saheed Soldiers and Swadhinata Sangrami (S.S.). The candidate has to produce attested copy/copies of the documentary evidence from the competent authority.

  9. The total number of students admitted from outside the State of Odisha (O.S.) to any course shall not exceed 5 % of the total number of sanctioned seats of the course. For the purpose of admission, an applicant will be deemed to be from within the State of Odisha, if he/she has passed the qualifying examination from any of the schools/ Universities situated within the territory of the State of Odisha. If an applicant whose parents are permanent residents of the state of Odisha, has passed his/her qualifying examination from school/University not situated within the territory of the state of Odisha, he/she may be considered to be from within the state of Odisha, provided he/she produces a Domicile certificate from a local Revenue Officer, not below the rank of the Tahasildar to the effect that his/her parents are permanent residents of that locality in the state of Odisha.

WEIGHTAGE FOR ADMISSION (For Degree Courses)

  1. NCC candidates holding 'B' and 'C certificate may get an additional weightage of 5% and 10% marks respectively over and above the aggregate marks secured by them in the qualifying examination subject to the condition that this additional weightage will not be given to an applicant who had failed earlier at Higher Secondary/Degree stage and has subsequently passed.

  2. Applicants who have represented the University or the State or the Central School/ Sainik School at the all India level in NCC activities will be given weightage of an additional 5% marks over and above the aggregate obtained by them subject to the production of the necessary certificate from the Director General of N.C.C./Director of N.C.C. of the sponsoring State which the applicant represented.

  3. The following categories of sports persons will be given admission as per the following norms subject to eligibility.
    • Those who have represented the country at the international level shall get straight admission.

    • Those who have represented the State at the national level shall get 10% weightage of examination.

    • NSS volunteers who have received State Award will be given weightage of' an additional 5% marks over and above the aggregate marks obtained by them in the qualifying examination.
      Best NSS volunteers at University level will be given weightage of an additional 3% marks over and above the aggregate marks obtained by them in the qualifying examination.
  4. Total weightage that can be availed is limited to 10 % of total marks.

Selection Process :

  • Final selection of the candidates for admission into different PG courses MFA and M Sc ETC (LE) will be made on the basis of their performance in the Entrance Examination and career assessment. The career assessment is based on the total marks secured by a candidate in the HSC, (Xth Class) or equivalent and H.S (XII Class) or equivalent, H.S.(XII Class) or equivalent and +3 examinations.

  • Final selection of the candidates for admission into different UG courses i.e. BBA and M Sc ETC (Int.) will be made on the basis of their career assessment.

The marks allotted for career assessment are as follows:

  1. For admission into BBA/M.Sc. ETC(Int) = H.S.C or equivalent – 20 and H.S. or equivalent – 30, Total = 50.

  2. For admission into MFA/M.Sc. ETC(LE) = H.S.C or equivalent – 10 and H.S. or equivalent – 15 and B.A/B.Sc./B.Com. – 23. B.A/B.Sc./B.Com.(Hons) – 23 + 10, and Distinction – 02, Total = 60.

Career marks are calculated as follows:

(Marks Secured without extra optional)/(Maximum marks without extra optional) X Marks Assigned for career

For a candidate who has passed the H.S.C/ H.S. or equivalent examination compartmentally, the average of two marks secured by him / her shall be taken into consideration for determining the aggregate. If a candidate is eligible for any weightage, such weightage marks will be added to the aggregate for career assessment.

In case the marks secured by two applicants are equal the merit shall be determined on the basis of marks secured by the candidate in the Entrance Examination.
